Why is it necessary to detect disease in plant ?
  1. Prevention of Spread:
    Early detection allows for prompt action to prevent the disease from spreading to other plants. Some plant diseases can spread rapidly through a field or garden, causing significant damage if left unchecked.
  2. Management and Control:
    Knowing which diseases are affecting plants enables farmers and gardeners to implement appropriate management and control measures.
  3. Food Security:
    With a growing global population, ensuring the health and productivity of plant crops is essential for food security. Detecting and managing plant diseases contributes to maintaining stable food supplies and preventing food shortages.
  4. Economic Impact:
    Plant diseases can have significant economic impacts on farmers, agricultural industries, and food supply chains. Early detection and management can help minimize these economic losses.
  5. Crop Protection and Yield Optimization:
    Plants affected by diseases often experience reduced growth, yield, and quality. Detecting diseases early allows for interventions that can protect crop yields and optimize production.
